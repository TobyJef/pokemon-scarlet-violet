New Features

With a new generation of Pokemon comes new species of Pokemon, new regional forms, and new features. There are some returning but altered features from previous main series or spin off titles to fit the new design of Pokemon Scarlet and Violet. The biggest of these newest features come in the form on a fully open world map allowing full player freedom for exploration.

The introduction of The Union Circle allows for up to four players the chance join up and share their Pokemon adventure together. Then there is the new Tera Raid dens, a built upon feature from the raids of Pokemon Sword and Shield. Test your type match up knowledge by challenging Pokemon that have a different base type to what you already know. Essentially creating the first tri typed Pokemon.

Smaller differences to mention depending on your version of the game, include the Academy name Naranja/Uva, the colour and style of the school uniforms, red accented uniforms for Scarlet and blue for Violet. Your Pokemon Professors will also be determined by what version you play which is a never before seen addition. The paradox Pokemon will vary also, will a preference for prehistoric or futuristic sway your choice? Unfortunately missing out for this generation is version exclusive Gym Leaders like in Pokemon Sword and Pokemon Shield which have not made a return.

The Open World

For our playthrough, the decision was made to explore the well sized open world around us, and get through the gyms and other over arching story lines in no particular order. With vast rolling fields, open seas, jungles, mountains and caves to explore, it is far too easy to spend your play session in getting lost and forgetting what you set out to do. Simply put the Paldea region is the largest map of any Pokemon game. We are still finding unchallenged static trainers to this day. The map although colourful, isn't the greatest however. Slightly sluggish to move through, no filter options and only four stages of a limited zoom it can make it difficult to quickly find what you are looking for.

Gym Battles and Gym Challenges

So the basic of Gym Battles remain intact. Defeat the Gym Leader and their team of preffered type Pokemon and recieve a badge. To be able to earn the opportunity to battle a Gym Leader however, players have to defeat the Gym Challenge first. To be honest, these challenges were a bit of a miss. At best these challenges are just a mini game. At worst it feels like content just to pad out the run time of the game. Ever fancied kicking a giant inflatable olive around an obstacle course and into an open goal under a time limit? No? Well too bad because you without it you can't progress your game. Sorry.

Once you suffer through the Gym Challenge and take on it's Leader, it's business as usual until the Gym Leader is down to their final Pokemon. Their chosen signature Pokemon is not what you expect as the Pokemon's typing does not conform with the rest of the Leaders team, presenting you with initally a surprise. However they will Terastalize their Pokemon from it's base type, to the Gym Leaders preffered typing. The first few times this happen did through us, as squaring off against a Rock Type in Sudowoodo in a Grass Gym is not par for the course, and instinct tricks you into wanting to switch Pokemon. However once realising that the typing has now changed from Rock to Grass, it's back to battling with confidence. A interesting new feature to add but one that could of maybe used more though to add a bit more of a challenge to the Gym Leader experience.

Exclusive Pokemon

As always each game has it's version exclusive Pokemon. In Scarlet and Violet within each edition there are 24 version exclusive Pokemon. These include the Legendary Pokemon that appear on the front cover of each game. Koraidon will be found in Scarlet and Miraidon in Violet.

There are 14 exclusive wild Pokemon, One trade evolution and Seven Paradox Pokemon that can be found later in Area Zero near the end of the story. There has already been an version exclusive limited timed raid event Pokemon for each game, Scarlet recieving Walking Wake and Violet recieving Iron Leaves. Rated as Five Star Raids, these Tera raids were a signficant challenge, even tougher than some Six Star Tera raids. Both raids were accessible to each game through joining hosted online raids from the opposite version.

To fully fill your Pokedex with the 400 Pokemon available in the game and get your hands on that sought after shiny charm. You will be required to either trade for those Pokemon unobtainable to your version, try your luck with a mystery trade or perhaps join an online tera raid party.

Tera Raids

One aspect where Terastalised Pokemon does hit home is in the new Tera raid battles. One of the built upon features from Pokemon Sword and Shield. The Tera raids add the most to Scarlet and Violet's replayability for after the end credits have rolled. The raids range in difficulty from One to Six stars with time exclusive Seven star raids which gradually introduce returning Pokemon that were not included in the base game. One star raids are perfect for new players and the daily Six star raids are for those trainers looking for a challenge. Where these raids shine is the chance to take on Pokemon with typings that are not its own, making you think more on not only your Pokemons strength and weaknesses but that of the Raid Pokemon also.

These raids can be attempted by yourself or with a group of friends or fellow online players. We do recommend to follow the general advice to give your team and yourself the best chance of success. Which is to raise your Pokemon to level 100, fully EV trained with items or the traditional way and that the Pokemon IV's are either Best or Hyper Trained. If you struggle with understanding the difference between your Pokemon IV's (Individual values) and EV's (Effort values) there are plenty of great resources online on this subject. However if you do find yourself struggling to come out on top remember to sign up to our newsletter as we cover the best strategies and Pokemon builds to bring along.

Picnics and Union Circle

Sandwiches and more

Picnics are the Paldean variation to Sword and Shields camping and cooking mechanic. Picnics are primarily used for two things. First is breeding, gone are the days at dropping two Pokemon off at the Pokemon Day care for the purpose of breeding Pokemon eggs. Either to breed eggs of fully evolved Pokemon down to their stage one forms to fill gaps in the Pokedex or to breed your way to Pokemon genetic supremecy and the coverted Six Best IV's and preffered nature or the rare chance of hatching a shiny Pokemon. However with Hyper training, mints to alter Pokemons natures, or suppliments. Breeding to prefection for personal or competitive use is no longer the only way. Providing your in game wallet is deep enough.

Purpose number two of the picnics is the new sandwich builder mechanic. Follow along your favourite in game recipe, place down the bread and layer on the toppings and finishing off the sandwich with a pick, to unlock a thirty minute game play boost. Want a better egg appearance and hatch rate? Make the Jam Sandwich. Want to encounter more Normal type Pokemon? Make the Fruit Sandwich. Each sandwich and it's fillings provided different boosts and at different levels, up to Level Three. With the more rare and expensive ingredients providing the better boosts. You can also purchase sandwiches and meals from different vendors around Paldea that can provide you with your desired boost. Although these boosts can only achieve up to Level Two, which can be enough for what you need, such as when breeding Pokemon during a picnic.

Union Circle

A fun yet flawed new feature, The Union Circle is a first for the Pokemon franchise and allows you and up to three friends all to gather into the same game at the same time while online. Roam the Paldea region together and if you are playing with a friend with the opposite version to yourself take turns setting the pace and encounter the exclusive Pokemon to each game. Don't forget to bring a Level 28 Palafin along and level up again for Palafin's Union Circle exclusive evolution.

The flaws however in this unique feature do not take long to appear. With online co-op communication between those involved is key. So first up being that if you want to explore Area Zero together. For design reasons unknown once players desecend into Area Zero, those involved in the Union Circle will no longer be visible to anyone in the party. So good luck trying to follow one another without any open dialogue. Expanding on this point, if the party is not together then the benefit of gaining access to version exclusive Poemon is lost until you find your invisible partners. Secondly and maybe most unfortunately. Playing the game while inside of The Union Circle, expect your frame rate and game performance to drop through the floor escpecially when playing with 3-4 other trainers.
