Our Review

Pokemon Scarlet and Pokemon Violet while staying true to the original Pokemon formula, by assembling your team of six Pokemon through catching, evolution or trading. Testing yourself against your rival, the eight Pokemon Gym Leaders and eventually the Elite Four to become Pokemon Champion. Have built further upon the foundations of what Pokemon Sword and Pokemon Shield have layed to continue the evolution of main series Pokemon games into Generation Nine. While some Pokemon traditionalists see these changes as disapointing because it takes the series further away from the design of earlier generation of games. We here at Pokeviews disagree with that sentiment, as the game franchise is fast approaching 25 years old. With the leaps in hardware and visual capabilities each new generation of rival consoles make and Nintendo consoles not being the same market leaders of the 1980's and 1990's, to compete against their Playstation and XBOX competitors. Nintendo focus on their best assest for selling consoles with their extensive list of first party games. So some tweaks and improvements need to be made to help keep the series fresh. Do all of these changes translate well? No, not all of them. But at least Nintendo and Gamefreak are attempting something new.

The Story

Welcome to Paldea, a region in the Pokemon universe which is styled and inspired by the geography and culture of Spain and Portugal. The second consecutive European country to be chosen following Pokemon Sword and Pokemon Shield's unique take on the United Kingdom.

After our character creation and introduction to Pokemon and the region by the version exclusive Pokemon Professor. Our adventure begins as always in the main characters homestead. This time in the small and sleepy village of Cabo Poco. You then are introduced to the Headmaster of Pladea's main school. The Naranja Academy if you are playing Pokemon Scarlet, or the Uva Academy if playing Pokemon Violet. Afterwards you not only meet the new starter Pokemon, but also your rival Nemona, the version exclusive Legendary Pokemon Koraidon or Miraidon and Arven the other main companion on your journey.

This is when Scarlet and Violet departs from tradition, gone are the linear path routes from one village, town or city to the next. We now have a fully open world to explore although our main story objective is to continue through Los Platos and towards the Academy, we cannot help but stray far and wide from the beaten path and see what the opening are of Paldea has to offer. After eventually making our way to the Academy and through the manadatory cutscenes and dialogue, we are now able to traverse the world with the box legendary as our ride mount to explore it with and through story progression Koraidon and Miraidon will gain abilites to Swim, Climb, Glide and Jump higher. Our main objective now, well whatever we want really. There are three interconnected story lines for us to follow at our choosing and in any order. Do we choose Nemona's story line in the quest to become regional champion? Or maybe Arven's story and follow the Path of Legends, or even take a trip towards Starfall Street to uncover the origins of Team Star and dismantle this troublesome group.

Whilst Victory Road story arc is not breaking any new ground, the Path of Legends is surprisingly touching and Starfall Street is a pleasant enough experience though slightly repetative. Overall the complete story is one of the stronger entries into the franchise.

The Starter Pokemon

As with Pokemon tradition, the Pokemon starters we are introduced to at the beginning of the game are the standard Grass, Fire and Water types. For those that are still unsure of basic type match ups. Fire trumps Grass, Grass trumps Water and Water trumps Fire. Our choice of Pokemon companions is between Sprigatito the green Grass type Cat. Fuecoco the red bipedal Fire type Crocodile or Quaxley the Water type white duckling with a bouyant blue hair do.

On Pokemon Scarlet we picked Fuecoco and Quaxley on Pokemon Violet. After checking the type match ups of all three starters and their evolution lines, against the Gym Leaders and Elite Four members Fuecoco with it's Fire typing is by far the best suited to take advantage of the upcoming type match ups. Quaxley and it's Water type has no type advantage against any of the eight Gym Leaders and could be considered for a more challenging playthrough.

Terrastalisation

In Pokemon Scarlet and Violet the new battle feature is the Terrastalise ability. Similar to gimmics from previous generations like Mega Evolutions and Dynamax ablities, this once per battle one Pokemon ability will transform your Pokemon to something that looks like it's from the Swarovski catalogue, and adds additional damage to moves of the same Terra type. Each Pokemon's own Terra type is based on it's typing. So single type Pokemon such as a wild Drowzee will always be the given Psychic Terra type ability. Dual type pokemon such as Tyranitar when caught in the wild will be asigned with either a Dark or Rock Terra type. You can find rare glistening Pokemon throughout the open world that will have a set randomised Terra type from it's normal typing. But through story progression the ability to change the Terra type of your Pokemon is unlocked, potentially giving you a key edge in battle if competitve play is your thing or a particular type advantage when participating in the Terra raid dens.

The Open World

During our first playthrough the decision was made to first explore the well sized open world around us, and progress through the story lines no particular order. With vast rolling fields, open seas, jungles, mountains and caves to explore, it is all too easy to spend your play session getting lost and forgetting what you initially set out to do. Simply put the Paldea region is the largest map of any Pokemon game, and we are still finding unchallenged static trainers to this day. The map menu feature although colourful, isn't the greatest however. Slightly sluggish, no filter option and a limited zoom in and out it can sometimes be hard to quickly find what you are looking for as even when zoomed out the map and it's icons can appear cluttered, by showing everything all at once on the part of the map area you are viewing. The size of the map is set to increase even further when the upcoming paid additional content of The Treasure of Area Zero is fully released.

Gym Battles and Gym Challenges

So the basic of Gym Battles remain intact. Defeat the Gym Leader and their team of preffered type Pokemon and recieve a badge. To be able to earn the opportunity to battle a Gym Leader however, players have to defeat the Gym Challenge first. To be honest, these challenges were a bit of a miss. At best these challenges are just a mini game. At worst it feels like content just to pad out the run time of the game. Ever fancied kicking a giant inflatable olive around an obstacle course and into an open goal under a time limit? No? Well too bad because you without it you can't progress your game. Sorry.

Once you suffer through the Gym Challenge and take on it's Leader, it's business as usual until the Gym Leader is down to their final Pokemon. Their chosen signature Pokemon is not what you expect as the Pokemon's typing does not conform with the rest of the Leaders team, presenting you with initally a surprise. However they will Terrastalise their Pokemon from it's base type, to the Gym Leaders preffered typing. The first few times this happen did through us, as squaring off against a Rock Type in Sudowoodo in Bug Gym is not par for the course, and instinct tricks you into wanting to switch Pokemon. However once realising that the typing has now changed from Rock to Grass, it's back to battling with confidence. A interesting new feature to add but one that could of maybe used more though to add a bit more of a challenge to the Gym Leader experience.

Performance

The games performance is one the most documented disapointing aspects of this generation. From game breaking bugs one recently as of May 2023, prompted the latest Terra Spotlight event to be cancelled and a apology statement being issued. There are multiple reported issues of the game crashing, save corruption, players falling through the world floor and Terra raid battles. Lists of issues seem to go on and on, and with a strange lack of patches being issued to help fix some of the bugs, it is not a good look from those involved at Nintendo and Gamefreak and they must do better with the development and release of Generation 10.

The Visuals

Visually the game is a bit of mixed bag, some views and biomes are quite well presented, while other assets have issues with textures and pop ins. The games mostly runs smoothly, but their are instances of frame rates dropping to near what can be described as single digits, a issue also encountered in Pokemon Legends Arceus. Also during Union Circle sessions where any more than two people playing can cause the game to really struggle. On a positive some Pokemon attack animation can be considered a step forward, but the majority is nothing new, and the combat style is a step backwards when compared to the Pokemon battles from Pokemon Legends Arceus.

Our final thoughts

Overall, Pokemon Scarlet and Pokemon Violet is a good entry into the Pokemon mains series franchise and we loved our playthroughs and continue to enjoy the game after completion of the post game. The difficulty of the Six and Seven star Terra raids keep us on our toes by giving new type match up problems to solve and how to best our opponent, making victory all the more satisfying. Warts and all we would recommend to any fan that they pick up a copy at some point and see what this generation has to offer.

However, in the idea of fairness. Generation Nine had multiple issues reported at launch and continues to do so now. It is debatable on where blame can be placed. Both the game at release and in it's current state is clearly missing some polish and could of benefited for another six to twelve months of development. Is there an argument to be had that some of the issues Pokemon Scarlet and Violet have encountered is down to the running capabilities on all variations of the Nintendo Switch and with the console nearing the end of it's life cycle. Could a decision of been made to hold back the release of Generation Nine and use it as a flag ship release title for a next generation Nintnedo console? Although when comparing Scarlet and Violet to the recent release The Legend of Zelda, Tears of the Kingdom. A game that had been announced and held back until it was deemed finished and acceptable to be released. Has rewarded fans of the series with another visually stunning game and another game of the year contender.

Pokemon Scarlet and Pokemon Violet could of been a Pokemon game above all the rest, to set a new bar for what the franchise and provide a Pokemon experience like no other. However due to the games many issues, more of the same old questions and concerns are raised. Our hopes for the future of the franchise is to truly take note of the fair cristicism Scarlet and Violet face. But to continue to build on what as worked well and with Generation 10 or whatever else is planned for Pokemon, most likely making it's debut on the next generation of Nintendo Console. Proper time can be taking to truly make a Pokemon game that only the fans can dream of.
